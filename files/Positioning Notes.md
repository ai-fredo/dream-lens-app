# Positioning Notes

Strategic notes on how DreamLens should describe its knowledge base, given what the research actually supports. This is a product-risk note as much as an editorial one.

## The problem with "authoritative"

The landing page and design spec describe the KB as "authoritative" and "grounded in established psychological traditions." That phrasing is a liability:

- The *established psychological tradition* on dream symbols is that they have **no fixed meaning** (Jung, CW 8 §471) and that dictionary interpretation is worthless (Jung, and every serious Jungian analyst).
- The *scientific tradition* (Domhoff) holds that no specifically Freudian/Jungian symbol claim is empirically supported.

So a KB that asserts symbol meanings *authoritatively* is claiming exactly the thing the authorities reject. A journalist, a psychologist, or a skeptical App Store reviewer can fairly call it pseudoscience. That's a reputational risk for a wellness brand.

## The stronger position

Reframe from **"we know what your dreams mean"** to **"we help you think about what your dreams might be working through."** This is:

- What the science actually supports (continuity hypothesis: dreams reflect waking preoccupations).
- What Jung actually practiced (amplification + the dreamer's associations).
- Already consistent with your own in-app disclaimer ("reflection, not therapy").
- A *harder to attack* and *more premium* position — thoughtful, not gimmicky.

## Suggested copy edits

| Current | Suggested |
|---|---|
| "authoritative" | "grounded in depth psychology and dream science" |
| "what your dreams really mean" | "what your dreams may be working through" |
| "interpret the underlying meaning" | "explore possible meanings" |
| "an algorithm that can tell the person about themselves" | "patterns that help you understand yourself" |

None of these weaken the pitch. They make it credible. "Explore what your subconscious is processing" sells as well as "decode your dreams" and can't be called fraudulent.

## The one genuinely defensible differentiator

Every competitor (DreamScript, MyDream, Shadow) pipes dreams to a raw LLM with no grounding source, producing inconsistent, confident-sounding guesses. DreamLens's real edge is the **grounded, consistent, transparent-about-its-lenses** interpretation. Lead with *that* — "interpretation you can trust to be consistent and honest about its reasoning" — not with a false novelty claim about memory or a false authority claim about meaning.
